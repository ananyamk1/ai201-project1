METRORail

METRORail is Houston’s light rail network, offering convenient access to many popular destinations in and around downtown Houston. These include the Texas Medical Center, Museum District, Houston Zoo, Theater District, NRG Stadium, University of Houston and Texas Southern University. With trains running frequently, you’ll be on board and on track to your destination quickly.

Ways to Pay

There are multiple options for paying your fare when you enter a METRORail platform, as shown below.


RideMETRO Fare Card Learn more
RideMETRO Fare Card

This card functions as a debit card for riding METRO. You can load and store money on it. Money will then be deducted each time you pay your fare.

If you have a Day Pass on your RideMETRO Fare Card, the validator will use the Day Pass first before using any money stored on the card. You cannot use a Day Pass on your RideMETRO Fare Card to ride Park & Ride bus service.

How To Pay

Tap your physical RideMETRO Fare Card on the fare validator, which can be found: 

  • Inside any local bus or Park & Ride bus, as you board
  • On all METRORail platforms
  • Inside any METRO curb2curb vehicles, as you board

Each customer must pay individually. The same payment method cannot be shared.

Make sure the validator screen shows a green checkmark when you tap. Your transfer expiration time and card balance will also appear on the screen.

Transfers

After the fare amount is deducted from your physical RideMETRO Fare Card, you'll receive free transfers between any of these METRO services for up to three hours:

  • Local bus
  • METRORail
  • METRO curb2curb

For transfers to Park & Ride bus service, you'll only pay the fare difference for up to three hours. You cannot use a Day Pass on your RideMETRO Fare Card to ride Park & Ride bus service.

You must tap your card each time you enter any vehicle or platform.

1 for 10

Paying your fare with a RideMETRO Fare Card entitles you to our 1-for-10 loyalty program – you’ll receive one free ride after every ten paid rides.

RideMETRO App Learn more
RideMETRO App

Use the app to create a free virtual RideMETRO Fare Card. It functions as a debit card for riding METRO. You can load and store money on it. Money will then be deducted each time you pay your fare.

If you have a Day Pass on your virtual RideMETRO Fare Card, the validator will use the Day Pass first before using any money stored on your card.

How To Pay

Scan the QR code in your RideMETRO app approximately six inches below any fare validator, which can be found:

  • Inside any local bus or Park & Ride bus, as you board
  • On all METRORail platforms
  • Inside any curb2curb vehicle, as you board

Each customer must pay individually. The same payment method cannot be shared.

Make sure the validator screen shows a green checkmark when you scan your QR code. Your transfer expiration time and card balance will also appear on the screen.

Transfers

After the fare amount is deducted from your virtual RideMETRO Fare Card, you’ll receive free transfers between any of these METRO services for up to three hours:

  • Local bus
  • METRORail
  • METRO curb2curb

For transfers to Park & Ride bus service, you'll only pay the fare difference for up to three hours. You can't use a Day Pass on your virtual RideMETRO Fare Card to ride Park & Ride bus service.

You must scan the QR code each time you enter any vehicle or platform. 

1 for 10

Paying your fare with a virtual RideMETRO Fare Card entitles you to our 1-for-10 loyalty program – you’ll receive one free ride after every ten paid rides.

RideMETRO Ticket Learn more
RideMETRO Ticket

3-Hour Pass and Day Pass tickets can be purchased from a Ticket Vending Machine (TVM), located on METRORail platforms, using:

  • Cash (no pennies; no bills higher than $20)
  • Credit or debit card (Visa, Mastercard, American Express, Discover)
  • Apple Pay or Google Pay

Passes are valid on local bus, METRORail and METRO curb2curb only.

How To Pay

Scan your 3-Hour Pass or Day Pass ticket under the fare validator, which can be found:

  • On all METRORail platforms
  • Inside any local bus, as you board
  • Inside any curb2curb vehicles, as you board

Each customer must pay individually. The same payment method cannot be shared.

Make sure the validator screen shows a green checkmark when you scan. Your transfer expiration time is printed on the ticket.

Transfers

After your 3-Hour Pass or Day Pass ticket is scanned, you'll receive free transfers between any of these METRO services for up to three hours:

  • Local bus
  • METRORail
  • METRO curb2curb

You can't use a 3-Hour Pass or Day Pass ticket to ride Park & Ride bus service. 

Credit / Debit Card Learn more
Credit / Debit Card

You can pay your fare with a credit or debit card. METRO accepts Visa, Mastercard, American Express and Discover.

How To Pay

Tap your credit or debit card on the fare validator, which can be found: 

  • Inside any local bus or Park & Ride bus, as you board
  • On all METRORail platforms
  • Inside any METRO curb2curb vehicles, as you board

Each customer must pay individually. The same payment method cannot be shared.

Make sure the validator screen shows a green checkmark when you tap. Your transfer expiration time will also appear on the screen.

Transfers

When paying with credit or debit card, you'll receive free transfers between any of these METRO services for up to three hours:

  • Local bus
  • METRORail
  • METRO curb2curb

For transfers to Park & Ride bus service, you'll only pay the fare difference for up to three hours.

NOTE: To receive free transfers, you must tap the same card on all fare validators throughout your trip. A physical card and a virtual version of the same card stored in Apple Wallet or Google Wallet count as different payment methods. 

Mobile Wallet Learn more
Mobile Wallet

You can pay your fare with a credit or debit card using Apple Pay or Google Pay. METRO accepts Visa, Mastercard, American Express and Discover.

How To Pay

Open Apple Pay or Google Pay on your smartphone or wearable device. Tap your smartphone or wearable device on the fare validator, which can be found: 

  • Inside the local bus or Park & Ride bus, as you board
  • On all METRORail platforms
  • Inside the METRO curb2curb vehicles, as you board

Each customer must pay individually. The same payment method cannot be shared.

Make sure the validator screen shows a green checkmark when you tap. Your transfer expiration time will also appear on the screen.

Transfers

When paying with Apple Pay or Google Pay, you'll receive free transfers between any of these METRO services for up to three hours:

  • Local bus
  • METRORail
  • METRO curb2curb

For transfers to Park & Ride bus service, you'll only pay the fare difference for up to three hours.

NOTE: To receive free transfers, you must tap the same card on all fare validators throughout your trip. A physical card and a virtual version of the same card stored in Apple Wallet or Google Wallet count as different payment methods.

Riding

First, please let riders exit the train before you board. After all riders have disembarked through the railcar doors, please enter the train and choose an open seat. Please avoid sitting in the priority seating areas for the elderly and people with disabilities, marked by the International Symbol of Access (Wheelchair Symbol).

All METRORail platforms are accessible under the standards of the Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA). They offer sloped ramp access from both ends of the platform and level boarding that easily accommodates mobility devices.

You'll need to tap or scan the fare validator as you enter a METRORail platform.

The following payment methods will need to be tapped on the fare validator:

  • Physical RideMETRO Fare Card
  • Credit or debit card (Visa, Mastercard, American Express, Discover)
  • Mobile wallet with Apple Pay or Google Pay

The following payment methods will require you to scan the QR code six inches under the fare validator:

  • Virtual RideMETRO Fare Card (in the RideMETRO app)
  • 3-Hour Pass ticket (not valid on Park & Ride)
  • Day Pass ticket (not valid on Park & Ride)

More details can be found on the All About Fares page.

Service animals

You’re allowed to travel with a service animal.

Service animal means any guide dog, signal dog, or other animal individually trained to work or perform tasks for an individual with a disability, and must be kept under the handler’s control.


Non-service animals (including emotional support animals)

You’re allowed to travel with non-service animals (pets) on METRO vehicles, but they must be transported and properly restrained in an animal carrier at all times.
